<p><p>The Analog and Mixed Signal IP market consists of two primary types: Firm IP and Hard IP. Firm IP, or soft IP, is a flexible design that allows customization and adaptation for specific applications, often provided as RTL code. In contrast, Hard IP is a pre-verified, non-customizable physical implementation, typically presented as a layout that is ready for integration into a chip. Each type serves distinct purposes in design efficiency, speed of integration, and customization needs within semiconductor development.</p></p>
