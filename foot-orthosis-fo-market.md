<p><p>The Foot Orthosis (FO) market comprises two primary types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to accommodate motion, providing support while allowing for natural foot movement, often used in sports rehabilitation and performance enhancement. In contrast, static orthotics aim to stabilize or immobilize the foot, focusing on corrective alignment and pressure distribution, making them essential for managing chronic conditions or post-operative recovery. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing foot function and overall mobility.</p></p>
