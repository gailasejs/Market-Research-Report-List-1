<p><p>Industrial wireless power transmission for short-range applications involves two primary market types: electromagnetic induction technology and magnetic field coupling technology. Electromagnetic induction utilizes alternating magnetic fields to transfer energy between coils, typically employed in charging stations for electric vehicles and industrial equipment. In contrast, magnetic field coupling relies on resonant inductive coupling, allowing for efficient power transfer over slightly greater distances with minimal losses. Both technologies enhance operational flexibility and safety, minimizing the need for physical connections in industrial environments.</p></p>
