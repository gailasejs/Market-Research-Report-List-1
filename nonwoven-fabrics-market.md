<p><p>The nonwoven fabrics market comprises several production types, each with distinct processes. Dry-laid nonwovens involve fibers being laid on a conveyor belt, then bonded by heat or adhesives, ideal for hygiene products. Spunmelt combines spunbond and meltblown methods, creating strong, lightweight fabrics used in medical and filtration applications. Wet-laid involves dispersing fibers in water, forming a mat that is dried, primarily used in industrial and specialty textiles. Other methods include needle-punched and chemical bonding, expanding the variety of applications in various industries.</p></p>
