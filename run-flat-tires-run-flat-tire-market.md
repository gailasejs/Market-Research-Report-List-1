<p><p>Run-flat tires are designed to maintain functionality after a loss of air pressure, allowing drivers to continue driving for a limited distance. There are two main types: self-supporting and support ring systems. Self-supporting run-flat tires have reinforced sidewalls that bear the vehicle's weight even when deflated. In contrast, the support ring system includes an additional ring that supports the tire's structure, enabling continued driving without air. Both technologies enhance safety and convenience by reducing the risk of being stranded due to tire failure.</p></p>
