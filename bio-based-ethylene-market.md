<p><p>The bio-based ethylene market encompasses three primary types of feedstocks: sugars, starch, and lignocellulosic biomass. Sugars, derived from crops like sugarcane and sugar beets, offer a direct fermentation pathway to ethylene. Starch sources, such as corn and potatoes, are converted into sugars before being processed into ethylene. Lignocellulosic biomass, sourced from agricultural residues and forestry byproducts, provides a more sustainable option, though it requires advanced processing techniques to extract fermentable sugars. Each type contributes to the growing demand for sustainable chemical production.</p></p>
